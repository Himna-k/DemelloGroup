Lender Compliance ... Items Most Lenders Check When Underwriting for Approval

The business finance pre-qualification and credit optimization sections

Below is what most lenders want to see that your business has completed. The system will provide you with the necessary instruction to complete all 20 items.

IMPORTANT NOTE: LENDERS DO NOT PORTFOLIO YOUR LOANS. What this means is that when they make a loan they sell the "loan paper" in the secondary market. For example when they make you a $10 loan they sell your loan paper for $12 so that they have the money to make more loans. In order for them to sell the loan paper your business must "conform". If they cannot sell your loan paper you will be declined. "Lender Compliance" are items that help your business "conform" so that your loan paper can be sold and therefore you can be "approved". This is why you need to complete all items of Lender Compliance.

Top 20 Compliance Items Lenders Review

We will be addressing each of these 20 items and making sure you have them completed.

1 Entity with State Records: Your entity must be open, active and in good standing.
2 Federal EIN: Your Employer Identification number filing must match your state filing exactly.
3 Bank Account: The day your business bank account opened is when lenders consider it began.
4 Business Licenses: All applicable city, county, state business licenses must be filed.
5 Business Physical Address: The business must have a business address as per the USPS database.
6 Unique Business Phone: The business must have an FCC listed business number, not cell or home.
7 411 Directory: Business phone number must be listed with 411 under the exact legal name.
8 Business Website: Business needs a website that is found on the local business searches and apps
9 Professional Business Email: Using a free email account for your business can get you declined.
10 Experian Business: File must be open with a good credit history and a strong score
11 Creditsafe: File must be open with a good credit history and a strong score.
12 Dun & Bradstreet: File must be open with a good credit history and a strong score.
13 Equifax Business: File must be open with a good credit history and a strong score.
14 Your Bank Rating: Your business bank rating should be a minimum of a low 5.
15 Vendor Credit Lines of Credit: At least 7 vendor lines of credit with payments made early.
16 Business Credit Cards: At least 5 business credit cards with payments made early.
17 Installment Credit Reporting: At least 3 installment trade-lines with payments made early.
18 Bank Loan or Equipment Lease: A business only bank loan or equipment lease with early payments.
19 Total of 15 Reporting Trade-Lines: At least 15 reporting trade-lines to build strong business scores.
20 Business Plan: An SBA loan compliant business plan with market analysis & revenue projections.
